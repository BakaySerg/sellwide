What Exactly is Keyword Research?

Keyword research is the skill and ability of determining the words, phrases, and intentions of prospective buyers who use Google's search engine. After doing keyword research, you may write, create, and optimize web pages that particularly target the keywords and phrases you discovered. The goal is to get high Google ranks and clicks to your website.

Why is keyword research important for SEO success?

The majority of web sites get no traffic since website owners do not write about topics that people are truly looking for. In other words, most individuals don't know how to do keyword research to lead their website's organic traffic strategy.

What Makes Our SEO Keyword Research Services Unique?

Our technique starts by gathering all possibly relevant keyword phrases and then narrowing that keyword universe down to a final selection of target keywords predicted to offer the highest return.

We examine the following factors:

Demographics of the target audience.

Understanding the characteristics and search habits of your target audience(s) is critical for picking keywords that will capture highly qualified prospects' attention.

Competitive Keyword Analysis:

The smaller volume phrase is sometimes more enticing.

A smaller rival bidding for an organic ranking position for a keyword sought by larger competitors may lack the necessary funding.

Keywords with a long tail:

Long tail keyword research discovers low volume terms with a high conversion chance and/or profitability.

For example, just a few customers may look for a certain kind of overhead crane — but if each transaction generates $1 million in income, it is definitely worth targeting.

Relevance:

The term "employee benefits seminars" may create a lot of traffic, but if a human resources outsourcing provider does not hold seminars, it may not be worth targeting since it cannot assist people searching for this service and hence does not line with user intent.

Number of searches:

A term that receives 40,000 monthly searches is far more likely to be successful than a comparable phrase that receives 4,000 monthly searches.

Specificity:

The term "packing" has a large volume, but its meaning is too wide to attract relevant queries.

In comparison, a term like "purchase packing boxes" has much lower volume but exceptionally high relevance and user intent strength.

User Placement:

If 35,000 of the 40,000 monthly searches for a certain term are from overseas users and your firm only sells in the United States, it is unlikely that it is worth targeting.

Keyword research for local SEO:

Keyword research for firms servicing local markets necessitates a different technique, one in which we are well-versed.
