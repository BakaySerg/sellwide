What Exactly Is Competitor Analysis?

Competitor analysis is often performed to analyze the strengths and weaknesses of other market participants,

define your company's position in respect to them, and expose gaps and assess your ability to fill them.

Many businesses begin competition research while developing a digital marketing plan and never return to it. This is a mistake, since a timely update on your competitors' online movements may often help you change your campaign mid-stream and help it perform better in the end.

FAQ

Who are your primary rivals who may have a significant impact on your business?

You must determine your primary rivals based on their effect or the influence they will have on your firm in the future. Make a list of the top 5 to 10 firms that you have identified as potential rivals.

What are the prospective rivals who are not presently competitors but may become such in the future?

You should also consider prospective rivals, or potential firms that aren't now competitors but might be in the future. Consider potential replacement items or innovations that may emerge in the future and compete with your existing offering.

What value do your rivals provide to their customers?

Consider the value that your rivals provide to their consumers. This might include product qualities, features, quality, advantages, issues, and so on.

How do your rivals get clients to purchase from them?

It is critical to understand the strategies and approaches used by your rivals to persuade their clients to purchase from them. Examine their call to action, their promises, and the language they use to approach and urge clients to purchase from them.

Where are they promoting their goods or services?

It is critical to understand and follow your rivals' advertising strategy and approaches. Answering this question will lead you to new advertising channels, as well as information on what your rivals are doing on them and how they express their values to their customers.
